## Abstract

Modern autonomous AI systems produce decisions at scale but produce trustworthy *confidence* about those decisions only by accident. Existing approaches &mdash; softmax probabilities, Dempster-Shafer evidence theory, naive Bayesian fusion, ensemble averaging, LLM self-evaluation &mdash; each fail in characteristic ways under realistic deployment conditions: miscalibration under correlated sources, overconfidence on out-of-distribution inputs, instability under semantically-equivalent reformulations, and silent susceptibility to adversarial perturbation of the confidence value itself.

This paper introduces **VERDICT WEIGHT**, a confidence-scoring framework built around an eight-stream composition rule. Five core streams produce a calibrated confidence value through evidence aggregation, uncertainty decomposition, temporal stability checking, cross-source coherence scoring, and post-hoc calibration. Three hardening streams provide adversarial-input detection, cryptographic provenance for every scoring event, and a registry-anchored kill switch for compromise conditions. The streams compose under a documented rule with veto priority for hardening signals and first-class abstention for contradictory evidence.

The paper presents the framework's design principles, the formal composition rule, and a preliminary empirical evaluation against standard baselines. It concludes with the case for a confidence layer as a distinct architectural component &mdash; separable from the model stack it scores &mdash; in any deployment where AI decisions carry consequence.

## Relationship to Papers 2 and 3

Paper 1 establishes the framework and the eight-stream composition. It is foundational rather than empirical: the core empirical work is in Paper 2, and the formal architectural treatment is in Paper 3.

| Paper | Role |
|---|---|
| **Paper 1: Foundation** | Framework introduction, design principles, composition rule. *(this paper)* |
| **Paper 2: Empirical hardening** | IEEE-grade benchmarks, ablations, calibration curves, real-world proxy validation. See [Paper 2](/papers/paper-2-empirical). |
| **Paper 3: Unified architecture** | Formal completeness proof, eight-stream coverage argument, composition soundness. See [Paper 3](/papers/paper-3-architecture). |
