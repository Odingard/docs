# Introduction

VERDICT WEIGHT is an open confidence synthesis framework for autonomous AI systems. It defines a mathematically rigorous methodology for combining signals from multiple independent sources into a single, calibrated, adversarially-robust confidence score.

**Current version: v3.0** — The specification is open and will evolve as the autonomous AI ecosystem matures. [Contribute on GitHub](https://github.com/Odingard/verdict-weight).

## What is VERDICT WEIGHT?

Autonomous AI systems — agents, RAG pipelines, intelligence platforms, fraud detection engines — ingest signals from multiple sources simultaneously. Today, most systems treat those signals with equal weight, or rely on a single primary source score.

VERDICT WEIGHT defines a four-stream confidence synthesis model that:

- Scores each incoming intelligence signal across four evidence dimensions
- Synthesizes those dimensions into three output metrics: **Signal Strength (SS)**, **Doubt Index (DI)**, and **Consequence Weight (CW)**
- Applies context-adaptive weight profiles tuned to specific operational domains
- Suppresses adversarially injected signals by **57.8%** compared to single-source baselines

VERDICT WEIGHT is not a product, library, or service you install. It is a **specification** that defines what a conformant confidence scoring system must do — and how to build one.

## The Confidence Gap

<Info>
The fundamental problem: autonomous AI systems cannot currently distinguish between a credible signal and a spoofed one. They act on what they receive. VERDICT WEIGHT provides the mathematical layer to change that.
</Info>

Modern autonomous systems face a structural vulnerability. An adversary does not need to compromise your model. They only need to inject a high-credibility signal with no independent corroboration. Without a confidence synthesis layer, that signal reaches the decision engine at full weight.

VERDICT WEIGHT closes this gap by making **corroboration the dominant predictive stream**. An adversary can spoof one source. They cannot manufacture independent agreement across four.

## The Four Evidence Streams

VERDICT WEIGHT evaluates every intelligence signal across four independent dimensions:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Source Reliability (SR)" icon="shield-check">
    Historical accuracy and trustworthiness of the originating source
  </Card>
  <Card title="Corroboration Count (CC)" icon="network-wired">
    Independent agreement across multiple unrelated sources
  </Card>
  <Card title="Temporal Decay (TD)" icon="clock">
    Recency and freshness of the intelligence relative to context
  </Card>
  <Card title="Historical Accuracy (HA)" icon="chart-line">
    Track record of the source's prior predictions in this domain
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Three Output Metrics

Every scored signal produces three outputs:

- **Signal Strength (SS)** — calibrated confidence in the signal's validity
- **Doubt Index (DI)** — inter-stream disagreement; elevated DI flags manipulation or noise
- **Consequence Weight (CW)** — actionable confidence score after doubt penalization
